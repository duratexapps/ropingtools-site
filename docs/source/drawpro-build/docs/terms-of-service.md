## 1. What Draw Pro Is

Draw Pro is software that helps producers run team roping draws and notify entrants
of their team assignments. Draw Pro does not organize, sanction, judge, or supervise
any event, and is not a party to any event's rules, payouts, or on-site operations.
The producer running the event is solely responsible for the event itself.

## 4. The Draw Process

- **Random pairing.** Where Draw Pro runs an automated draw, header/heeler pairing
  among solo entrants is random, constrained only by the producer's stated
  classification cap. Draw Pro does not guarantee any particular pairing, running
  order, or outcome.
- **The cap rule is absolute.** No pairing — automated or manually entered by a
  producer — will be created if the combined classification exceeds the event's
  stated cap. This rule cannot be waived by a producer, an entrant, or Draw Pro staff.
- **Unmatched entrants.** If the algorithm cannot find a valid partner for an entrant
  within the field as entered, that entrant may require an additional, unpaid run to
  complete the draw ("extra run"), or may need to be manually paired by the producer
  consistent with the cap rule. See the Refund & Extra-Run Policy for how fees are
  handled in this case.
- **Manual overrides.** A producer may manually adjust the draw sheet after the
  automated draw runs. Every manual override is logged, together with the producer's
  acknowledgment, for accountability purposes. Draw Pro is not responsible for a
  producer's manual pairing decisions, only for enforcing the cap rule against them.
- **Run-order spacing.** Draw Pro attempts to space a repeat entrant's runs at least
  ten (10) teams apart. Where field size makes this mathematically impossible, Draw
  Pro flags the conflict for the producer rather than guessing; the producer is
  responsible for resolving it.

## 5. Entry Information You Provide

You're responsible for the accuracy of the information you (or, for scanned-card
entries, the office staff entering it on your behalf) provide, including your name,
classification number, and contact information. Entering a false or outdated
classification number to gain an unfair pairing is a violation of these Terms and
may be reported to the relevant governing body.

## 6. Notifications

Draw Pro notifies entrants of their team number and partner assignment by email
(and, in the future, SMS if you've opted in and provided a phone number). Delivery
is not guaranteed — if you don't receive a notification, it is your responsibility
to check with event staff for your team number and run order.

## 7. Scanned Card Entries

Where a producer's office staff scans a handwritten entry card, that image and the
information extracted from it are handled per the Privacy Policy. Staff verify and
correct machine-read (OCR) information before it's used in the draw, but Draw Pro
does not guarantee the office staff's transcription is error-free; entrants should
confirm their own information is correct wherever it's displayed to them.

## 8. Minors

Where an entrant is a minor, a parent or legal guardian must be the one submitting
the entry and agreeing to these Terms, and is responsible for the accuracy of the
information submitted and for the minor's participation in the event itself. See the
