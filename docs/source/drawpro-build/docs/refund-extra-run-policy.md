> **This is a starting draft, not final legal language.** This policy exists

## 1. Unmatched Entrants ("Extra Runs")

If Draw Pro's automated draw cannot find a valid partner for a solo entrant within
the cap constraints of the field as entered, that entrant may end up completing the
event via an additional, unpaid run rather than a paid entry — a known limitation
of blind random-draw matching when the field doesn't divide evenly.

- **This entrant is not charged an additional entry fee for the extra run.**
- **The producer does not collect the entry fee they would have for that pairing**,
  since no second paying partner existed to complete it. This is a real cost to the
  producer, not to Draw Pro or to the entrant — see Section 3.
- Draw Pro does not compensate producers for this shortfall. It is a structural
  outcome of blind-draw matching that Steer Me (pre-formed partner matching) is
  intended to reduce over time, not something Draw Pro's fee model offsets directly.

## 2. Manually Paired Entrants

Where a producer manually pairs an otherwise-unmatched entrant (per the Terms of
Service's manual override provision), normal entry fee handling applies as if that
pairing had been drawn automatically — the cap rule still applies, and no fee waiver
applies simply because the pairing was manual rather than automated.

## 3. Producer-Side Financial Responsibility

who is unmatched. **Setting entry fees, collecting them, and handling any refund a

## 5. Disputing a Draw Outcome

An entrant who believes their draw result was affected by an error — a card
misread during scanning, an incorrect classification number on file, or a manual
override they believe violated the cap — should raise it with the producer running
